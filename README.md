# Retail Analytics Case Study

## Overview
This project focuses on a retail company experiencing stagnant growth and declining customer engagement. By leveraging SQL-based data analytics on sales transactions, customer profiles, and inventory data, this project provides actionable insights to optimize inventory and improve customer retention.

## Business Problems
- **Product Performance Variability:** Identifying high and low-performing products to optimize inventory and marketing focus.
- **Customer Segmentation:** Addressing the lack of clear customer classification to enable targeted marketing.
- **Customer Behavior Analysis:** Analyzing repeat purchase patterns and loyalty indicators to enhance retention strategies.

## Objectives
- Perform data cleaning and Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using SQL.
- Identify top/bottom products to streamline inventory management.
- Segment customers based on total quantity purchased:
  | Total Quantity Purchased | Customer Segment |
  | :--- | :--- |
  | 0 | No Orders |
  | 1 - 10 | Low |
  | 10 - 30 | Mid |
  | > 30 | High Value |
- Derive insights on loyalty and purchase frequency.

## Datasets
- **Sales Transaction:** Transaction details, customer IDs, product IDs, quantity, and dates.
- **Customer Profiles:** Demographic data including age, gender, location, and join date.
- **Product Inventory:** Product details, categories, stock levels, and pricing.

## Technologies
- **Language:** SQL
- **Focus:** Data Analytics, Business Intelligence
